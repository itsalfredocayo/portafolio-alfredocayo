# Auditoría de Ecosistema: Corey Haines
**Plataforma:** LinkedIn
**Filtro Etnográfico:** Transición hacia el marketing operacional impulsado por agentes de IA locales (Claude Code, Cursor), automatización de contexto y la evolución de "roles" a "capacidades" sistémicas.

---

### Sistematización de Contexto de Producto para Agentes de IA
* **Señal Técnica:** Eliminación de fricción en flujos de trabajo AI mediante la inyección automatizada de contexto base (Single Source of Truth).
* **Contenido Crudo:**
> I built a skill for Claude Code that creates a product marketing context document so every other marketing skill already knows your product.
> 
> You run it once and it captures everything — what your product does, who it's for, how you're positioned, who your competitors are, what makes you different, and your pricing. It saves to a file that all 32 marketing skills read automatically. So when you use /copywriting or /page-cro or /email-sequence, they already know your product, your audience, and your voice without you explaining it every time.
> 
> Set it up once, never repeat yourself again. It's the foundation everything else builds on. It's called /product-marketing-context and it's part of Marketing Skills — a free, open source collection of 32 marketing skills for AI agents like Claude Code, Cursor, and Codex.

### Arquitectura Estructural de Campañas Paid Ads vía IA
* **Señal Técnica:** Framework para delegar la estructuración de embudos pagados (intención, CPAs, negativas) a sistemas autónomos para evitar fugas de presupuesto.
* **Contenido Crudo:**
> I built a skill for Claude Code that structures paid ad campaigns so you stop wasting budget on the wrong setup.
> 
> You tell it your product, budget, and goals, and it builds the full campaign architecture — ad groups by intent level (branded, competitor, problem-aware, solution-aware), daily budgets for each, target CPAs, keyword strategies, and negative keyword lists. It knows that branded searches should be cheap and competitor terms should be profitable, not just running.
> 
> Most startups throw money at Google Ads with one ad group and wonder why ROAS is terrible. Structure is everything.

### Auditorías CRO Automatizadas y Priorización de Impacto
* **Señal Técnica:** Uso de skills en Claude Code para diagnósticos rápidos de Landing Pages enfocados en cuellos de botella de conversión y Quick Wins.
* **Contenido Crudo:**
> I built a skill for Claude Code that audits any landing page and tells you exactly why it's not converting.
> 
> You give it a URL and it comes back with a full CRO breakdown — your headline is too vague, your CTA says "Learn More" instead of something people actually want to click, your social proof is buried below the fold where nobody sees it, your pricing is hidden on a separate page when it should be right there on the landing page.
> 
> Every issue gets a priority level and an expected impact estimate so you know what to fix first. Most pages have 3-5 quick wins that can double conversions without a redesign.

### El Fin del Humano como Intermediario (Middleman)
* **Señal Técnica:** Reflexión conductual: el valor del operador B2B pivota de "ejecutar/transferir datos" a "diseñar sistemas, contextos y barreras de contención (guardrails)".
* **Contenido Crudo:**
> With newer AI workflows is that you are no longer the middleman → This is actually great news ↓
> 
> The old flow was simple. Ask AI for outputs, copy the result, move it into another tool, adjust it, repeat. Now systems like Claude Code can do the work directly. That changes everything (for real this time).
> 
> Once AI can move between tools on your behalf, the value shifts away from manual coordination and toward setting better direction, better guardrails, better context, and creating better systems. The job becomes less about carrying work from one place to another and more about deciding what should happen in the first place.
> 
> That is a much more interesting kind of work. And it is going to reshape how a lot of teams operate.

### De Roles a Capacidades (El Operador Sistémico)
* **Señal Técnica:** Evolución antropológica del mercado laboral B2B; la capacidad de orquestar herramientas abstractas vence a las etiquetas de puesto tradicionales.
* **Contenido Crudo:**
> Hot take: I think we are moving from a world defined by roles to a world defined by capability.
> 
> The old labels still exist, but they are getting less useful. Marketer. Developer. Designer. Strategist. What matters more is whether you can think clearly, direct tools well, and produce outcomes.
> 
> The people who thrive will probably be the ones who keep expanding their range instead of defending the borders of their title. What are you bringing to the table? Can you operate outside of your job description?
