**Plataforma:** LinkedIn
**Filtro Etnográfico:** Gestión del liderazgo en Marketing, superación de la fricción en la adopción de IA corporativa (AI Readiness Gap) y metodologías estructuradas de validación de mercado (Needs Analysis).

### La Responsabilidad Ineludible de la Alineación de Liderazgo
* **Señal Técnica:** La alineación entre áreas (Ventas, Marketing, CS) no es un subproducto, es la métrica principal de éxito y responsabilidad del líder.
* **Contenido Crudo:**
> Not sure why it’s hitting me hard today but there’s one thing that matters more than anything when it comes to your success as a leader.
> 
> Alignment with your peers.
> 
> It helps you manage up. It helps protect your team’s focus. It helps protect your sanity, because misalignment will do more damage to your psyche than d**gs.
> 
> Walk into a leadership meeting where sales, marketing, and customer success are clearly not working together. It’s so clear and uncomfortable. And hey, that’s on you. You are the one failing. Not your peer. Not your boss. It’s you. Alignment is part of the job. I’d argue one the MOST importamt parts of the job.
> 
> It really boils down to two things:
> 1. Be a decent human.
> 2. Communicate regularly with each other.
> 
> If the other person isn’t willing to reciprocate, it’s your responsibility to fix it. If that doesn’t work… it might be a larger issue that forces change.

### Framework para Análisis de Necesidades (Needs Analysis)
* **Señal Técnica:** Guía operativa de 4 pasos para extraer información procesable (Pain points, Objeciones, USPs) directamente del mercado antes de ejecutar estrategia.
* **Contenido Crudo:**
> Leadership teams of any size should be utilizing a needs analysis whenever building strategy around product or entering a new market. Marketing leaders should be obsessed with it.
> 
> Here's what you need to start:
> - A list of 30 buyers, customers and lost prospects to interview. You are only going to net 10-15 completed surveys.
> - 10-15 questions that can be answers over the phone or by email.
> 
> Recognize Pain Points and Triggers:
> - Where do they have pain?
> - What would trigger them to buy now?
> - What KPI’s are they trying to improve? 
> 
> Overcome Their Buying Concerns:
> - What else are they considering?
> - What’s holding them back from buying? 
> 
> Highlight the Unique Value of Your Company:
> - What features do they care about?
> - How do they describe your company?
> - What are your USPs? 
> 
> Counter Any Lingering Doubts:
> - What would they want to see in the product?
> - What other barriers exist?

### La Falacia de la Adopción de IA (The AI Readiness Gap)
* **Señal Técnica:** El problema de la IA en B2B no es la adopción individual, sino la falta de infraestructura organizativa para absorber el exceso de output (rendimiento) generado por los "heavy users".
* **Contenido Crudo:**
> Calling all marketing leaders: Most of you probably think our problem with AI is adoption. It's not. 
> 
> 91% of organizations haven't used AI to fundamentally change how they work. They've deployed tools, run pilots, applied to specific workflows... but they're not ready to fully embrace this new tool. 
> 
> There are a handful of people who got faster with AI than the rest of the team could keep up with. We are talking about more output... a ton more output. More output using AI isn't a win if you haven't built the infrastructure and bandwidth to support it. We call it the AI Readiness Gap.
> 
> We think we're leading, but we are just getting further ahead of our people. AI readiness isn't measured by what you can do. It's measured by what your team can absorb.

### Intervención Operativa: Blackout Week & AI Build Day
* **Señal Técnica:** Táctica para contrarrestar la "ansiedad de IA" y fomentar la experimentación forzada mediante la cancelación de reuniones y la dedicación exclusiva a resolver flujos de trabajo.
* **Contenido Crudo:**
> Calling all leaders: I know some of us are stuck in AI anxiety that's affecting every area of our lives. You are waiting for the dust to settle before you move.
> 
> Complaining is a voluntary form of brain damage. Every time we focus on what's not working, the "salience network" starts tagging problems as more important than progress. The antidote? One word: "Good."
> 
> We stopped waiting, and we call it Blackout Week with an AI Build Day. 
> 
> Once a month, the Docebo marketing team clears every internal meeting. No standups. No syncs. No Slack. Just deep work.
> 
> And we added an AI Build Day. Every person blocks a full day, picks one real workflow problem, and uses AI to build and solve it. We are asking the team to document what happened to share at some point in the future. 
> 
> Call me optimistic, but I believe the teams that win aren't waiting. They're already getting messed up, hitting CRON walls, waiting to "fork," saying "good," and moving forward anyway. Give your team the space to say "good" and build with them.
