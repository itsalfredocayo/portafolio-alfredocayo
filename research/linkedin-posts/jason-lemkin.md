### Cómo sobrevivir en un mercado SaaS saturado (El vacío de los $100M)
* **Señal Técnica:** Framework de entrada al mercado (GTM) para startups: atacar los segmentos "despreciados" por los unicornios (sub-nichos, verticales o rezagos arquitectónicos).
* **Contenido Crudo:**
> How Can A SaaS Startup Survive In A Crowded Market? Find a segment of the market to win in. Where you can build a 10x feature, and then a 10x solution, that solves a niche problem that customers will pay for.
> 
> As Bigger SaaS companies cross $100m in ARR, and then $1B in ARR, they stop focusing on smaller niches. They might give up on SMBs, or freemium, or higher churn segments. But you might be able to do well there.
> 
> Many Bigger Cos aren’t vertical-specific. There can be room for a vendor focused just on e-commerce, or healthcare, or contact centers. For example, Mailchimp is very strong in general email but Klaviyyo is worth $4B doing email for e-commerce.
> 
> Most Bigger Cos are 10+ years old. Do something important they don’t do well. Maybe they don’t integrate with newer vendors. 10+ year old platforms were architected for a different age.
> 
> Another way to look at it is any vendor sort of has to ignore any market segment that is less than 10% of their revenues. As SaaS leaders cross $1B+ in ARR... that leaves a $100m+ of customers for each of them they just aren’t that focused on.

### La cruda realidad de los Agentes de IA en B2B ($7M Rev / $300k Spend)
* **Señal Técnica:** Desmitificación del "Plug-and-Play" en IA B2B; el éxito no depende del modelo (LLM), sino de la integración humana experta (FDE) y la supervisión operativa diaria.
* **Contenido Crudo:**
> We run 20+ AI agents at SaaStr. They've generated over $7M in revenue. We spend $300K a year on AI agents.
> 
> The single biggest variable in whether an agent works is not the model. Not the prompt. It's whether you get a great Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE) helping you set it up. A human from the vendor who sits with you, gets into your data, learns your workflows, fixes the broken stuff.
> 
> Let me give you a real example. We deployed an AI SDR tool. The setup was: here's your login, here's a knowledge base article, good luck. The first emails it sent were unusable. Tone was wrong. Context was wrong. Without someone from the vendor sitting with us, we would have churned in 30 days.
> 
> We got it working. But only because we spend 30+ minutes every single day reviewing output, tuning prompts, fixing edge cases. Most companies don't have that.
> 
> The irony is brutal: the companies that need FDEs most (SMBs, startups, lean teams) are the ones least likely to get them. One major AI vendor we use now only provides FDEs to companies with 5,000+ employees.
> 
> If you're wondering why your AI agents aren't performing... ask yourself: Did you get a real FDE? Or did you get a login and a Loom?

### Comportamiento Ejecutivo en Tiempos de Crisis (CEO Psychology)
* **Señal Técnica:** Contraste conductual de liderazgo en startups; la correlación directa entre la transparencia operativa (presencia) y el crecimiento.
* **Contenido Crudo:**
> What I see CEOs at struggling start-ups do:
> - Go dark
> - Hide
> - Lash out
> - Tweet about lots of things non-work
> - Invest time on tiny expenses
> - Argue internally
> 
> What should do:
> - Be Present
> - Be Honest
> - Be the Rock
> - Be Realistic/Optimistic
> 
> You just plain grow faster that way
